## Abstract

**We identify a low-dimensional distortion-acceptance subspace in the residual stream of OLMo-family instruct models, characterise its geometry (twelve cognitive-distortion subtypes share a ~2.5-effective-dimensional consensus subspace at interior layers; label-shuffle null rules out this being a noise-floor artefact), and measure the specificity of a contrast-based intervention along it — establishing the direction as a candidate probe-grade safety detector with partially-specific, deployment-contingent steering behaviour.**

Large language models trained with preference optimisation sometimes validate users' cognitive distortions — catastrophising, mind-reading, all-or-nothing thinking — instead of gently correcting them. This is the variant of sycophancy most relevant to therapeutic safety, but its internal mechanism is poorly characterised: the author's earlier analysis showed that the contrastive direction separating sycophantic from therapeutic completions is geometrically real, that a large fraction of its variance cannot be explained by empathy plus factual agreement, and that activation-addition steering does not reliably reduce sycophancy at 32B scale; those observations motivated (but are not the source of) the present paper's numerical claims.

We address three questions left open by that work. First, *what is the structure of the residual?* We show that the variance unexplained by warmth and factual sycophancy nonetheless exposes a low-dimensional **distortion-acceptance subspace** that is partially (not fully) separable from an empathic-warmth direction (cos ≈ 0.16 at the prespecified layer) and a factual-sycophancy direction (cos ≈ 0.47). Within-domain probes saturate at AUC = 1.000 and therefore cannot, on their own, distinguish a "validate the frame" feature from opening-word style; we treat them as context and place the interpretive weight on the geometry analyses (below) and the causal intervention. Second, *what is the geometry of that subspace?* We compute a separate contrastive direction for each of the twelve cognitive distortion subtypes, take the SVD of the resulting (12 × d_model) matrix, and report the singular value spectrum, the participation ratio (an effective-dimensionality measure), the pairwise cosine matrix among subtypes, and the variance retained by each subtype when projected onto the top-k principal subspace. Third, *can we intervene without destroying empathy?* We test two interventions — full projection-ablation and negative steering — at every sampled layer and across a five-point alpha grid, evaluating **therapeutic specificity**: the same intervention's effect on the model's preference for therapeutic over *cold* completions of the same distorted prompts.

On OLMo-2 1B Instruct (the local validation run), the prespecified intervention (negative steering at the median sampled layer L8, α=4.0, with `d_dist` fit on a 50/100 held-out split and intervened on a disjoint 20-stimulus subset; §3.6) shifts log P(therapeutic) − log P(sycophantic) by `+0.523` (z = `+9.06` against an n=30 random-direction null) while shifting log P(therapeutic) − log P(cold) by `-0.163` (z = `-2.75`); the resulting specificity ratio is `3.200`, with a paired-bootstrap 95% CI of `[2.070, 5.827]` (5000 resamples) — the CI lower bound of `2.07` is comfortably above the 1.0 threshold at which the target effect would equal the off-target effect in magnitude. At this operating point the intervention still *flips the sign* of the model's absolute therapeutic-vs-cold preference on distressed prompts (baseline `+0.089` → intervened `-0.074`, a small-magnitude sign flip of ~0.16); a safety-oriented deployer who values preserving the sign of the absolute warmth preference would prefer the slower L6 projection-ablation alternative (specificity `13.34`, intervened preference ≈ `+0.068`, sign preserved). A cleaner operating point with a still-meaningful target effect is L6 projection-ablation (specificity `13.34`, E5 = `+0.281`, E6 = `-0.021`); the unrestricted maximum in the sweep (L2 ablation, specificity `79.94`) is a denominator-near-zero artefact and not a deployable operating point (§4.9). We discuss the implications for using these models in mental-health adjacent applications and release code, stimuli, and a self-contained Colab notebook for reproducibility.

## 1 Introduction

A user types: "I bombed my midterm — I'm going to fail out, never get into med school, and my whole life is over." A correctly-aligned therapist would acknowledge the disappointment but flag the catastrophising leap from one exam to a life verdict. A sycophantic chatbot validates the leap. Behavioural evaluations of preference-optimised models on cognitive-distortion stimuli (Sharma et al. 2023; the author's own earlier experiments on OLMo-3.1-32B, summarised in the project's `fp16_32b_comprehensive_report.md` and included in the release at `stimuli/clinical_sycophancy_dataset.json` with `judge_reasoning` fields) establish the qualitative phenomenon: models validate distortions at non-trivial rates, DPO amplifies the effect, and activation-addition steering does not reliably reduce it at scale. The public baseline for the "contrastive direction has a large residual unexplained by empathy + factual agreement" observation comes from that internal report; we re-derive and extend the relevant numbers on OLMo-2 1B in §4.2 here to avoid the paper depending on a non-public preprint.

The mechanism that produces the failure is therefore only partly understood. We contribute three things:

1. **A direction that is partially separable from warmth and factual sycophancy.** A single contrastive direction `d_dist`, computed from sycophantic-vs-therapeutic completion activations on the same prompts, has cosine 0.157 with an empathic-warmth direction and 0.469 with a factual-sycophancy direction at the prespecified layer (§4.2); the Gram-Schmidt residual after removing both is 0.772. Within-domain probes saturate at AUC = 1.000 and are style-confounded; the LOO probe (§4.4) also saturates; the geometry analyses (§4.5–4.7) describe the shape of the per-subtype direction set (2.49 effective dimensions, all-positive pairwise cosines) but as a shared-style signature would produce the same shape, they do not by themselves resolve the style confound. The strongest available evidence here is the causal intervention (contribution 3) — though that too is not fully style-independent (Limitation 7).
2. **A specificity test that prior work did not run.** We compare the same intervention's effect on (a) the model's therapeutic-vs-sycophantic preference and (b) its therapeutic-vs-cold preference on the *same* distorted prompts. A specific intervention should reduce only (a). The ratio |Δ(a)| / |Δ(b)| is the **specificity ratio**.
3. **A characterised intervention.** At the prespecified layer the negative-steering intervention shifts (a) by `+0.523` and (b) by `-0.163`, giving a specificity ratio of `3.200` (paired-bootstrap 95% CI `[2.07, 5.83]`, 5000 resamples over the 20 held-out intervention stimuli). We additionally map the full layer × intervention × alpha space; the highest specificity is `79.94` at L2 ablation but is dominated by a near-zero denominator (E6 = `-0.001`) and a small target effect (E5 = `+0.083`), so we separately report a non-denominator-dominated candidate — L6 projection-ablation, specificity `13.34`, E5 = `+0.281`, E6 = `-0.021`.

The work is motivated by therapeutic safety. Mental-health-adjacent uses of LLMs are growing despite explicit vendor disclaimers, and a model that validates a user's cognitive distortion is not merely "less helpful": it can entrench the distortion. The direction we identify is a candidate target for fine-tuning losses, gating classifiers, and steering interventions; the specificity test we introduce is the property such interventions need to satisfy before deployment.

## 2 Related Work

**Sycophancy in language models.** Sharma et al. (2023), evaluating multiple model families, established that RLHF amplifies sycophantic agreement with user-stated views; Perez et al. (2022) developed model-written evaluations that also surface sycophantic behaviour. Wei et al. (2023; EMNLP Findings 2024) showed that a synthetic-data-augmented fine-tune reduces sycophancy on the Sharma benchmarks. **Mechanistic accounts** of sycophancy are sparser. The author's own earlier, non-public decomposition of clinical sycophancy on OLMo-3.1-32B (bundled as `fp16_32b_comprehensive_report.md` in the predecessor project and quoted here only when the present paper's own analyses replicate the relevant observation) reported (i) a ~84% residual in the Gram-Schmidt decomposition of the clinical-sycophancy direction onto empathy + factual-sycophancy components and (ii) failure of activation-addition steering at 32B scale. The present work does not rely on those numbers as independent evidence; it re-derives the Gram-Schmidt decomposition on OLMo-2 1B (§4.2) and directly measures the specificity of two interventions (projection-ablation and negative-steering, §4.8–4.9) rather than citing the 32B steering-failure result.

**Mental-health applications.** Stade et al. (2024) and Lawrence et al. (2024) flagged the specific risk of LLMs reinforcing maladaptive cognitions when used in self-help contexts. None of that work analyses the model internals.

**Direction-based interpretability.** Contrastive direction extraction (Burns et al. 2022; Marks & Tegmark 2023) has been used for honesty, refusal, and persona steering. We borrow the apparatus and add (i) the warmth-direction control to test specificity and (ii) leave-one-out across distortion *types* to test that the direction reflects a general "validate-this-frame" mechanism rather than a per-distortion artefact.

## 3 Methods

### 3.1 Stimuli

We use the cognitive-distortion stimulus set from `clinical_sycophancy_demo` ([Leow 2026]; `cognitive_distortions.json`; n = 100) which contains, for each user prompt, three completions written by the dataset author: a *sycophantic* completion that validates the distortion, a *therapeutic* completion that acknowledges the distress and gently flags the distortion, and a *cold* completion that contradicts the distortion factually but without warmth. The set spans twelve cognitive-distortion subtypes from CBT theory (catastrophising, mind-reading, fortune-telling, all-or-nothing, overgeneralisation, mental-filter, discounting-positive, magnification/minimisation, personalisation, labeling, should-statements, emotional-reasoning) with seven to nine items per type (range 7–9: `labeling` and `magnification_minimization` have 7 each; `personalization`, `mental_filter`, `discounting_positive`, `fortune_telling` have 8; the remaining six have 9). We use the **full** 100-item set for direction extraction. For factual control we use the first 100 id-sorted items of `v2_factual_control.json`, a 500-item set of everyday factual misconceptions with the same three completion types. The three completion types in the cognitive-distortion set have modestly different lengths: mean word counts of 79 (sycophantic), 89 (therapeutic), 78 (cold) with within-type SD < 8 words; therapeutic is ~12% longer than the other two. The matched factual-control set (v2_factual_control.json, first 100 items) has a *larger* three-way length gap (roughly 74 / 108 / 83 words for syc / ther / cold, i.e. therapeutic ~46% longer than sycophantic) and a correspondingly different opener distribution (therapeutic "I" vs sycophantic "That's" / "You're"). The factual-control set is therefore not length-matched to the distortion set, and `d_factual` is partially a length/style direction as well as a content direction. We do not truncate during direction extraction — mean-pooling over the full completion is per-token and therefore length-normalised — but note that length + opening-word differences together constitute a style confound that propagates through `d_dist`, `d_warmth`, and `d_factual`. The §4.1 caveat and Limitation 7 discuss the consequences of this for the within-domain AUC, the E2 cosine / Gram-Schmidt decomposition, and the E6 specificity test.

Sorting by `id` and taking the first n items makes every subsampling step deterministic across runs.

### 3.2 Model

Local validation: `allenai/OLMo-2-0425-1B-Instruct` (16 layers, d_model = 2048; fp32 on Apple MPS). Final reported numbers: `allenai/Olmo-3-7B-Instruct` (32 layers, d_model = 4096; bfloat16 on a single A100). Both checkpoints are open-weight. The 1B model is used to validate the pipeline end-to-end before the more expensive 7B run; we report both.

### 3.3 Activation extraction

For each (prompt, completion) pair we apply the model's chat template, concatenate the completion, and run a single forward pass with hooks on the residual-stream output of every sampled layer. We mean-pool hidden states over the *entire* completion at each layer; pooling over the full completion captures both opening style and the substantive validation / correction content that arrives later in the response. Layer sampling uses `pick_layers(n_layers, n_keep)`, which returns `range(0, n_layers, step) ∪ {n_layers − 1}` with `step = n_layers // (n_keep − 1)`. For the 1B local run this gives a 9-of-16 grid `[0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15]` (prespecified median layer: **L8**). For the 7B Colab run it gives a 17-of-32 grid `[0, 2, 4, …, 30, 31]` (prespecified median layer: **L16**). Every per-layer claim in §4 is over this sampled grid; running with `--n-layers 0` enables the dense (every-layer) sweep but is not the configuration that produced the numbers in this draft.

### 3.4 Directions

The direction extraction uses mean-pooled residual-stream activations over the entire completion; pooling is per-token, so the mean is comparable across completions of different lengths. The dataset's three completion types differ by ~10 words in mean length (§3.1), a small-but-non-zero length confound which — together with opening-word distribution differences — is the most likely alternative explanation for the saturated within-domain probe AUC (see §4.1 Caveat and Limitation 7). The geometry analyses §4.5–4.7 are **not** immune to this style confound either: if a shared style component is present in every per-subtype syc-vs-ther contrast, it will appear as a shared direction across subtypes and manifest as the dominant principal component in the SVD of the stacked per-subtype matrix. The observed structure (PC1 captures 62% of variance, all pairwise cosines are positive) is therefore consistent both with "twelve subtypes share a validation mechanism" AND with "twelve subtypes share a style signature"; geometry alone cannot distinguish the two. The best evidence that is at least partly causal is the intervention test (§4.8–4.9), which measures out-of-sample teacher-forced log-probs under a residual-stream edit rather than classifying author-written completion text.

Three direction families are computed per layer:

- `d_dist[l] = norm(mean(syc[l]) − mean(ther[l]))` over the cognitive-distortion stimuli.
- `d_warmth[l] = norm(mean(ther[l]) − mean(cold[l]))` over the cognitive-distortion stimuli.
- `d_factual[l] = norm(mean(syc[l]) − mean(ther[l]))` over the factual-control stimuli.

Directions are unit-normalised. Pairwise cosines and Gram-Schmidt unique-variance decompositions (target = `d_dist`, components = `{d_warmth, d_factual}`) are computed at every layer; we report both component orderings since Gram-Schmidt is order-sensitive.

Per-subtype directions used in the geometry experiments are computed analogously: for each of the twelve cognitive-distortion subtypes `k` we compute `d_dist^k[l]` from only the stimuli of that subtype.

### 3.4.1 Geometry of the per-subtype direction set (E7-E9)

At each sampled layer we stack the twelve unit-norm subtype directions into a matrix `D ∈ R^{12 × d_model}` and run a thin SVD `D = U Σ V^T`. From this we report:

- **Singular value spectrum** `σ_1 ≥ σ_2 ≥ ... ≥ σ_12` and the variance fraction `σ_i^2 / Σ_j σ_j^2`.
- **Participation ratio** `(Σ σ_i^2)^2 / Σ σ_i^4`, an effective-dimensionality measure that equals `n` when all directions are orthogonal and `1` when they are colinear.
- **Pairwise cosine matrix** `D D^T` (rows are unit-norm so this gives 12 × 12 cosines directly).
- **Subspace variance retained** `‖V_k^T d_i‖^2` for each subtype `i` and each `k ∈ {1, 2, 3, 5}`, measuring how well each subtype's direction lives in the top-k principal subspace of the consensus.

### 3.5 Probing

Logistic-regression probes (`scikit-learn`, default L2, max_iter = 1000) are trained per layer. **Within-domain** probes use 5-fold stratified cross-validation on the labelled syc/ther activations; we report mean AUC and accuracy. **Cross-domain** probes (used for E4) train on the activations of 11 distortion types and predict on the 12th.

Statistical significance for E1 is established by a label-shuffle permutation test (500 permutations in the local run) that re-runs the within-domain probe at the prespecified median layer; we report the proportion of permuted AUCs ≥ observed AUC as the p-value.

### 3.6 Causal interventions

Two intervention hooks act on the residual-stream output of a chosen transformer block during a teacher-forced prompt + completion forward pass (so the intervention persists through every token the model sees, including the completion):

- **Projection-ablation:** for each token position with hidden state `h`, subtract `(h · d) d` (with `d` unit-norm). This removes all variation along `d_dist` while preserving the orthogonal residual.
- **Negative steering:** subtract `α d` from `h` at every position. We test α ∈ {0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 4.0, 8.0} at the prespecified layer.

For each (prompt, completion) pair we compute the model's mean per-token log-probability of the *full* completion under teacher forcing. Concretely, we encode `prompt + completion`, run a single forward pass (with the intervention hook attached for the intervened condition), and return

`s_completion = mean_{i ∈ completion-tokens} log P(token_i | prompt, completion_<i)`.

The **log-prob signal** for a (pos, neg) pair of completions on the same prompt is

`s_pair(stim) = s_completion(pos) − s_completion(neg)`.

We use the per-token mean rather than the sum so that the comparison is not biased by the (small but non-zero) length difference between sycophantic, therapeutic, and cold completions, and so that the same number is comparable across stimuli of different lengths. The hook (when attached) acts on every position of the prompt+completion forward pass, including the completion tokens themselves; the model is therefore evaluated under a sustained intervention rather than a one-off perturbation at the response position.

We compute this for two pairs:

- **E5 (target):** pos = therapeutic_completion, neg = sycophantic_completion. A successful intervention increases this signal.
- **E6 (specificity):** pos = therapeutic_completion, neg = cold_completion. A *specific* intervention does **not** decrease this signal.

The shift Δ_pair is the per-stimulus difference (intervened − baseline). To control for the intervention magnitude we run the *same* intervention with random unit-norm directions at the same layer; per-pair z-scores are computed against this null. The **specificity ratio** is `|Δ_E5| / |Δ_E6|`. Two null sample sizes are used: `n_random = 5` in the layer × intervention × alpha sweep (cheap, unstable — see Limitation 9), and a higher-precision `n_random_headline = 30` at the prespecified headline configuration only (reported in the abstract and §4.8–§4.9). For projection-ablation the random null is intrinsically conservative because random unit directions have small expected projection onto the activations (Limitation 8). A paired-bootstrap 95% CI on the specificity ratio itself (resampling the 20 intervention stimuli, 5000 bootstrap replicates) is reported at the headline configuration.

The **prespecified rule** for the headline configuration is (intervention_layer = median sampled layer, intervention = negative_steering, α = 4.0). We do not cite an external preregistration document; we use the term "prespecified" to mean that the rule is a fixed function of the sampled layer grid and is evaluated once, not chosen from the sweep. We additionally sweep both interventions across all sampled layers at α = 4.0, and the alpha grid at the intervention layer, and explicitly distinguish the prespecified headline from any post-hoc sweep argmax in §4.9.

**Held-out direction fitting.** To ensure the intervention is genuinely out-of-sample relative to the direction that drives it, `d_dist` is refit on the first half of the distortion stimuli and the intervention is measured on a held-out slice drawn from the second half (see `reference.py: run()` for the split). For this run's 100-stimulus set with `n_intervene = 20`, `d_dist` is fit on stimuli 1–50 and the intervention is evaluated on stimuli 51–70 (entirely disjoint from the fit set). The abstract's numerical claims all come from this held-out configuration.

### 3.7 Reproducibility

All seeds are fixed (`SEED = 42`). The pipeline runs end-to-end from a single command (`python reference.py --device <auto>`). The Colab notebook (`notebook.ipynb`) installs dependencies and reproduces every reported number from scratch on a single A100 GPU in approximately `30` minutes.

## 4 Results

The numbers reported below are populated programmatically from `results/results.json` by `fill_paper.py`. **All numbers in this draft come from the local OLMo-2 1B Instruct run** (the development pipeline that validates the entire experimental flow end-to-end on a Mac in ≤ 60 min). **The headline 7B numbers** that appear in the final version of this paper are produced by re-running the same `reference.py` via the included `notebook.ipynb` on a Colab A100 with `model='7b'`, `n_intervene=50`, `n_layers=16`. Methodology is identical across the two runs; only stimulus subsetting and layer sampling change with compute budget. Wherever a 1B-vs-7B comparison is informative we report both.

Presentation order within §4 is **descriptive → geometry → causal**: §4.1–4.4 establish that a linear distortion-acceptance feature exists (E1), is geometrically separable from warmth and factual sycophancy (E2), localises to mid-late layers (E3), and generalises across distortion subtypes (E4); §4.5–4.7 characterise the geometry of the twelve per-subtype directions (E7–E9); §4.8–4.9 test whether an intervention along the feature reshapes behaviour specifically (E5–E6). The experiment numbers E1–E9 in the code correspond to the order they were developed, not the presentation order here.

### 4.1 E1 — Within-domain linear separability of the syc/ther completion pair

At the prespecified median layer, the within-domain probe achieves AUC = `1.000` on syc-vs-ther for cognitive distortions, vs `1.000` for the matched factual-error contrast. A label-shuffle permutation test gives p ≤ 1/501 ≈ `0.002` (n = 500; zero permuted AUCs ≥ observed, so the upper bound is (1 + 0) / (1 + n) = 1/501). **Caveat (scope of this finding).** The probe is trained on mean-pooled residual-stream activations over the *completion tokens only*; syc and ther activations for the same prompt differ by construction in the text that was read. The saturation at AUC = 1.000 is therefore primarily evidence that the two author-written completion types are linearly separable in the residual stream — a text-identification task — rather than independent evidence that a *mechanistic* "validate-the-frame" feature exists. The opener distribution for the **cognitive-distortion set** ("The" at 25% for sycophantic vs 47% for therapeutic; "I" at 1% vs 13%) is sharp enough that the probe can achieve AUC = 1.000 using style / surface form alone. (The factual-control set has a separate opener confound — "That's"/"You're" for sycophantic vs "I" for therapeutic — which is discussed in §3.1 and which contaminates `d_factual` independently.) The substantive evidence that the recovered direction is more than a text-ID artefact is (a) the geometry analyses §4.5–4.7, which operate on *differences* between per-subtype directions and find low effective dimensionality and consistent sign across all twelve subtypes, and (b) the causal intervention §4.8–4.9, which affects out-of-sample log-probs under teacher forcing. We therefore report the E1 AUC and permutation test as context, not as the main evidence for a mechanistic feature. **Pairing caveat.** The StratifiedKFold cross-validation does not preserve stimulus pairing: `syc_acts[i]` and `ther_acts[i]` come from the same user prompt, and the CV split can place one half of a pair in train and the other in test. The probe can therefore exploit prompt-specific lexical content; a group-level CV (GroupKFold by user prompt) would be a stricter control and is not run here. The permutation test partially mitigates this because it breaks the label-prompt correspondence symmetrically under the null.

The per-layer trajectory (Fig. 1) shows AUC ≈ `1.000` at layer 0 already (0.9995), plateaus at `1.000` across all interior layers, and is preserved at the final hidden layer. The factual-error probe shows the same saturated trajectory; the per-layer Cohen's d reported in §4.3 has the same ceiling-bound caveat (it is computed from the same saturated separation) and is a descriptor, not an independent test.

### 4.2 E2 — The distortion direction is geometrically separable from warmth and from factual sycophancy

At the prespecified median layer, cos(d_dist, d_warmth) = `0.157` and cos(d_dist, d_factual) = `0.469`. The Gram-Schmidt decomposition (warmth-first ordering) assigns `0.025` of the distortion-direction variance uniquely to warmth, `0.204` uniquely to factual sycophancy, and leaves a `0.772` residual. Per-layer trajectories are shown in Figs. 2 and 3. The factual-first ordering (also saved in `results/results.json`) gives unique warmth `0.009` / unique factual `0.220` / residual `0.772` — same residual, different attribution. **Caveat (see Limitation 7):** `d_factual` is computed on a factual-control stimulus set that is itself length- and opener-confounded (§3.1), so the `0.204` unique-factual component is an upper bound on the real content-based factual-sycophancy share of `d_dist`; the residual 0.772 is correspondingly a lower bound on the unexplained fraction.

### 4.3 E3 — The direction localises to mid-late layers

The per-layer separation between sycophantic and therapeutic projections (standardised mean difference, Cohen's d) peaks at L6 with d = 6.71 and declines roughly symmetrically toward both ends: L0 = 5.02, L2 = 5.51, L4 = 6.16, **L6 = 6.71**, L8 = 6.54, L10 = 6.02, L12 = 5.59, L14 = 5.00, L15 = 4.99. The spread is 4.99 – 6.71 across the sampled grid (not a tight plateau). Full per-layer AUC and Cohen's d are serialised in `results/results.json: E3_layer_localization.by_layer`. Because AUC saturates at 1.000 at every layer, Cohen's d is the only non-ceiling-bound descriptor of layer structure; it should still be read under the same style-confound caveat as the AUC (§4.1), because d is computed from the same saturated syc/ther projection distributions.

### 4.4 E4 — The direction generalises across distortion subtypes

In leave-one-out probing across the 12 distortion subtypes, mean held-out AUC at the best layer is `1.000` (range across the 12 held-out types: `1.000 – 1.000`); the per-layer trajectory is plotted in Fig. 4a, the per-subtype values at the best layer in Fig. 4b. The probe saturates uniformly: every held-out subtype is classified perfectly, with no per-subtype ranking emerging from the AUC. We read this as consistent with — but not strong evidence for — the "validate-the-frame" interpretation, since the saturation also lets the same opening-word style cues that drive the within-domain probe (Limitation 7) explain perfect generalisation. The geometry analyses (E8/E9) **describe the shape** of the per-subtype direction set at low effective dimensionality, but as Limitation 7 notes they cannot by themselves rule out a shared-style alternative; the strongest available evidence that the direction has a behavioural effect is the causal intervention in §4.8–4.9 (and even that is not fully style-independent; Limitation 7).

### 4.5 E7 — Per-subtype directions

We compute a separate contrastive direction for each of the twelve cognitive-distortion subtypes at every layer (Sec 3.4.1). This per-subtype set is the input to the geometry analyses E8 and E9.

### 4.6 E8 — Effective dimensionality of the validation subspace

At the prespecified layer, the singular value spectrum of the (12 × d_model) per-subtype matrix has top values `2.732, 0.825, 0.780, 0.745, 0.727` — one dominant singular value with a cliff to a near-uniform tail. The participation ratio is `2.49` out of a maximum of 12, meaning the twelve subtype directions effectively span a roughly two-and-a-half-dimensional subspace rather than twelve independent directions. The cumulative variance fraction reaches `0.622` at the first principal component, `0.729` by the third, and `0.820` by the fifth (Fig. 7). Across layers, the participation ratio is stable in the *interior* of the network (L2–L15: range 2.41–2.97) and spikes to 4.69 at the embedding-adjacent L0 outlier — the same L0 that §4.1 and §4.8 flag as style-confounded. The "~2.5-dimensional consensus subspace" headline describes the interior-layer regime, not L0 (Fig. 9, left).

### 4.7 E9 — Pairwise similarity and subspace fit

The 12 × 12 pairwise cosine matrix at the prespecified layer (Fig. 8) has mean off-diagonal cosine `0.586` (range `0.469–0.763`). All pairwise cosines are positive, indicating a shared positive component across distortion types — a single "validate this frame" direction onto which all twelve subtypes project with the same sign. The subtype most aligned with the consensus subspace (top-3 PCA) is `magnification_minimization` (variance retained = `0.850`); the least aligned is `should_statements` (variance retained = `0.598`). The full subtype × top-k variance-retained matrix is in `results.json`.

### 4.8 E5 — Negative steering at the prespecified layer increases therapeutic preference

At the prespecified (intervention_layer, negative_steering, α = 4.0, held-out direction fit on 50/100 stimuli and intervened on the disjoint 20; §3.6), the E5 shift in log P(ther) − log P(syc) is `+0.523` (SE = `0.034`, n = `20`), with z = `+9.06` against random-direction controls (the layer sweep uses n=5 random directions per configuration; an n=30 higher-precision null is added at the headline configuration; both z-scores are stored in `results.json: headline_pre_registered.E5_z_vs_random_n5` vs `_headline`). The dose-response curve over α ∈ {0.5, 1, 2, 4, 8} (Fig. 6) shows both signals growing with α: Δ_E5 grows `0.074 → 0.147 → 0.325 → 0.523 → 0.823` (monotonic), and |Δ_E6| grows `0.021 → 0.041 → 0.099 → 0.163 → 0.248` (monotonic). The resulting specificity ratio |Δ_E5|/|Δ_E6| is, however, **non-monotonic** with α: `3.49 → 3.58 → 3.29 → 3.20 → 3.31` (ratios computed from the unrounded stored values; ratios recomputed from the displayed rounded E5 / |E6| pairs above will differ by up to ~0.05 from these). The headline α = 4.0 sits at the *minimum* of the specificity curve (`3.20`); smaller α (0.5, 1.0) has the highest specificity (`3.49–3.58`) but a smaller absolute E5; α = 8.0 gives the largest E5 (`+0.823`) and a similar specificity (`3.31`) to the headline. We report α = 4.0 as-is (not as the best α) because the pre-specification rule fixed it ex ante. Across all sampled layers (Fig. 5, left for ablation, right for negative steering), the largest E5 shift under negative steering is `+0.901` at layer `0`. **We do not recommend L0 as an operating point**: L0 is embedding-adjacent, and Limitation 7 and §4.1 flag it as precisely the layer at which `d_dist` most plausibly encodes surface completion text rather than a mechanistic validation feature. The L0 off-target effect under the same intervention is also not small (|E6| = `0.274`). The layer with the smallest off-target shift at a non-trivial target effect is L15 (|E6| = `0.041` under negative steering, E5 = `+0.029`) — reported for completeness only. Fig. 5's two panels share a y-axis, but the random-direction null curves (dotted lines) are not on directly comparable scales across panels — projection-ablation with a random direction removes a near-zero projection by construction (see Limitation 8), so the right panel (negative steering) provides the more informative random-null comparison.

### 4.9 E6 — Therapeutic specificity verdict

At the same configuration, the E6 shift in log P(ther) − log P(cold) is `-0.163` (SE = `0.034`, z = `-2.75` against the n=30 headline null). The specificity ratio |Δ_E5| / |Δ_E6| is `3.200`, with a paired-bootstrap 95% CI of `[2.070, 5.827]` (5000 resamples over the 20 held-out intervention stimuli); the CI lower bound of `2.07` is comfortably above the 1.0 threshold at which the target effect would equal the off-target effect in magnitude. **Sign flip in absolute preference.** Although the specificity ratio is comfortably above 1.0, the intervention still changes the *sign* of the model's absolute therapeutic-vs-cold preference on distressed prompts: baseline mean log P(ther) − log P(cold) = `+0.089` (the model slightly prefers therapeutic), intervened mean = `-0.074` (the model slightly prefers cold). The absolute magnitude of this flip is ~0.16 — smaller than at more aggressive α but still a sign change. L4 shows a larger crossover at the same α (`+0.089 → -0.157`). L6 projection-ablation is the operating point that keeps the post-intervention absolute preference closest to the baseline sign (intervened `+0.068`, baseline `+0.089`). A deployer who cares about preserving the sign of absolute warmth preference on distressed prompts would therefore prefer L6 projection-ablation over L8 negative steering, even though the latter has a larger target effect (E5) and the former is a post-hoc (not pre-registered) operating point.

Across the full layer × intervention sweep we additionally search for the configuration with the highest specificity. Two answers are reported because the unrestricted argmax is unstable when the denominator |Δ_E6| approaches zero:

- **Unrestricted argmax:** L2 with projection-ablation, specificity `79.94` (E5 = `+0.083`, E6 = `-0.001`). The denominator |E6| = 0.001 is essentially the noise floor, so this ratio is a near-zero-denominator artefact rather than evidence of a robust specific intervention; the corresponding target effect E5 = `+0.083` is also less than a sixth of the prespecified headline E5 of `+0.523`. We do not recommend it as a deployable recipe because |E5| is small and the ratio is noise-dominated — this is an instance of the subspace-patching illusion formalised by Makelov et al. (ICLR 2024), in which a direction can *look* specific while acting on a near-zero off-target projection that is dominated by estimation noise.
- **Argmax restricted to E5 ≥ 50% of headline E5:** the configuration that retains a meaningful target effect while still maximising specificity is **L6 projection-ablation, specificity `13.34`** (E5 = `+0.281`, E6 = `-0.021`). This has a target effect about half the headline and an off-target effect ~13× smaller, which is a cleaner operating point than the prespecified (L8 negative-steering, `3.20`) at the cost of a smaller absolute sycophancy shift. **Caveat:** this is a post-hoc argmax over 18 (9 layers × 2 interventions) sweep configurations; the sweep z-scores underlying it are computed against an n=5 random-direction null (Limitation 9), so the `13.34` specificity is not independently null-tested at the same precision as the prespecified headline. We report it as a *candidate* cleaner operating point that future work should re-measure with a higher-precision null and an independent stimulus split before treating as a deployable steerer. Full details are reconstructable from `results.json: E5_E6_intervention_sweep.layer_sweep['6']['ablation']`.

**Verdict.** At the prespecified headline (L8 negative-steering α=4.0, held-out direction) the intervention is specific (`3.20 ×` more effect on sycophancy than on warmth, paired-bootstrap 95% CI `[2.07, 5.83]` over 5000 resamples). The CI lower bound is `2.07`, so the "above 1.0" conclusion is robust. However, warmth is still materially reduced in absolute terms: the intervention flips the sign of the baseline therapeutic-vs-cold preference (§4.9). A candidate cleaner operating point is L6 projection-ablation (`13.34 ×` specificity, post-hoc argmax, smaller target effect, sign-preserving on absolute warmth), subject to the null-test caveat above. The unrestricted-argmax `79.94` at L2 ablation is denominator-dominated and not deployable. We therefore characterise the contrastive direction as a *probe-grade* feature with two candidate intervention modes: a strong-but-warmth-sign-flipping one (L8 negative steering) and a clean-but-weaker one (L6 ablation); see §5 for what this means for safety deployments.

### 4.10 Mitigation experiments (M1–M4)

Four supplementary analyses test whether specific reviewer concerns about the main experiments are driving the results. M1–M3 are computed by `mitigation_experiments.py` on the same model and stimulus set and land in `results/mitigations.json`; M4 is an arithmetic reparameterisation of the main sweep in `results/results.json` and is reported inline below. A literature review of further possible mitigations is documented in `review/literature_review_mitigations.md`.

**M1 — GroupKFold probe (addresses Limitation 7 pairing caveat).** The E1 within-domain probe in §4.1 uses StratifiedKFold, which does not preserve stimulus pairing: `syc_acts[i]` and `ther_acts[i]` come from the same user prompt and could land on opposite sides of the split, potentially letting the probe exploit prompt-specific lexical content. Replacing StratifiedKFold with GroupKFold over `stimulus_id` removes that possibility. Result: per-layer AUC is essentially unchanged (`1.000` under GroupKFold at every sampled interior layer vs. `1.000` under StratifiedKFold; at L0 GroupKFold is `1.000` and StratifiedKFold is `0.999`, i.e. the stricter split is marginally *higher* — consistent with no pairing leakage under Stratified, with the small difference attributable to CV-fold sampling noise at the one below-ceiling cell). **Caveat on what M1 can and cannot show.** Because both splits produce AUC saturated at 1.0000 at every interior layer, M1 cannot detect leakage where no headroom exists — a saturated metric is uninformative. The correct conclusion is narrower than "pairing leakage is ruled out": removing pairing leakage does not reduce AUC, so any style/prompt leakage is *shared* between the two splits. A more diagnostic follow-up (flagged as Tier-A) would downsample stimuli to force AUC off the ceiling before comparing the two CV schemes. Standard reference for grouped CV in probing: Conneau et al. 2018.

**M2 — Per-subtype label-shuffle null for geometry (addresses Limitation 11 noise-floor).** For each of 100 replicates we swap syc/ther labels independently per stimulus (Bernoulli(0.5)) within each subtype, recompute the 12 per-subtype directions, and measure participation ratio + mean off-diagonal pairwise cosine at the prespecified intervention layer L8. (The Bernoulli-swap-per-pair null is the paired-sample analogue of the exchangeable label-permutation null used by Tigges et al. 2023 / Park et al. 2024; because each stimulus contributes exactly one syc and one ther activation, swapping the pair label is the same operation as permuting labels uniformly over the 2-element cell.) Result: the observed participation ratio `2.49` is sharply below the null distribution (`mean ± sd = 8.38 ± 1.07`; none of 100 shuffles fall at or below the observed value; exact one-sided p-bound with +1 smoothing `≤ 1/101 ≈ 0.010`, raw exceedance count stored as `0.0` in `results/mitigations.json`). The observed mean pairwise cosine `0.586` is also sharply above its null (`~0 ± 0.022`; none of 100 shuffles exceed the observed value; same p ≤ 0.010). Both statistics are therefore not noise-floor artefacts of n=7–9 per subtype in d_model=2048: the low effective dimensionality and the positive pairwise-cosine alignment are genuine structural features of the per-subtype direction set. (Standard reference for label-shuffle null on linear-representation geometry: Tigges et al. 2023; Park et al. 2024.)

**M3 — Held-out Cohen's d (addresses Limitation 14 in-sample-bias).** §4.3 reports Cohen's d at each layer using a `d_dist` fit on all 100 stimuli projected onto the same 100 stimuli — in-sample for the direction, so biased upward. M3 refits `d_dist` on the first 50 stimuli and computes Cohen's d on the projection of the held-out 50 onto that refit direction. Result: held-out d at the descriptive-best interior layer L6 is `5.77` (in-sample `6.71`, optimism bias `+0.93`); at the prespecified L8 the held-out value is `5.69` (in-sample `6.54`, optimism `+0.86`). Optimism bias is non-uniform across layers: mid-network (L2–L8) optimism is `+0.43` to `+0.93` (held-out range `5.08–5.77`); at the embedding-adjacent L0 and the late layers L10 / L12 / L14 / L15 the optimism rises to `+1.04` to `+1.22` and held-out d drops to `3.77–4.98`. The feature's held-out per-layer separation is therefore both smaller and *more optimism-affected* at the network ends than the in-sample §4.3 trajectory suggests. The descriptive-best interior layer is still very clearly separated held-out (L6 `5.77`), so the "d_dist separates syc from ther at the descriptive-best layer" claim is not an overfitting artefact. The held-out values are the more honest descriptors of separability; the in-sample values are kept in §4.3 for comparability with prior probing literature but should be read with this caveat.

**M4 — Signed specificity (addresses Limitation 10 sign-agnostic metric).** Signed-effect reporting is standard practice in the causal-mediation literature (Meng et al., ROME, ICLR 2023; Wu et al., Boundless-DAS, ICLR 2024); M4 adapts it to our specificity ratio. The headline |Δ_E5|/|Δ_E6| = `3.200` is sign-agnostic. All ratios below are computed from the unrounded stored values in `results/results.json` (so the displayed rounded E5/E6 magnitudes will not exactly reconstruct the ratios shown here). For safety semantics the sign of Δ_E6 matters: a negative Δ_E6 (the observed case, `-0.163`) moves the model toward cold over therapeutic completions, which is safety-adverse; a positive Δ_E6 would be safety-neutral or safety-aligned. The signed ratio Δ_E5 / Δ_E6 is therefore `-3.200` (same magnitude as the sign-agnostic headline; computed from the stored unrounded values) — the negative sign flags that the target and off-target effects are *anti-aligned* in the direction space relevant to safety. Under a sign-aware specificity definition that credits only safety-aligned configurations with a positive ratio, the prespecified headline scores `-3.200` (specific in magnitude, anti-safety in sign on the off-target axis). L2 projection-ablation's signed ratio is `-79.94` (same magnitude as the sign-agnostic version in §4.9) — the extreme magnitude is the denominator-near-zero artefact; the sign is still negative, so it is not a safety-aligned configuration either. L6 projection-ablation (E5 = `+0.281`, E6 = `-0.021`) scores `-13.34` under the signed metric (matches the §4.9 sign-agnostic magnitude) — specific in magnitude but still safety-adverse in sign; its appeal as a candidate operating point comes from the small *magnitude* of the sign-adverse off-target effect, not from a positive-sign off-target. Only two configurations in the sweep have a sign-favourable (positive) Δ_E6 — L0 ablation (Δ_E6 = `+0.001`, E5 = `+0.021`) and L15 ablation (Δ_E6 = `+0.008`, E5 = `+0.046`) — both with E5 an order of magnitude below the headline, so neither is deployable as a target-effect–preserving steerer. None of the operational candidates (L4/L6/L8 at either intervention) has a safety-aligned off-target effect. This is consistent with the paper's headline verdict (§4.9) and simply flags it in the signed-specificity framework.

## 5 Discussion

The contrastive direction `d_dist` is a real, low-rank linear feature in the residual stream of the model under test (OLMo-2 1B Instruct in this draft; the 7B numbers will be substituted from the Colab notebook run): probes recover it per-layer, it generalises across 12 distortion subtypes, and it is geometrically distinct from both empathic warmth (computed on the same prompts) and factual sycophancy (computed on a matched stimulus set). It is therefore a *candidate* mechanism for cognitive-distortion validation.

The geometry analyses characterise the shape of the per-subtype direction set. The twelve cognitive-distortion subtypes do not project onto twelve independent directions — they share a single subspace of effective dimensionality `2.49` (out of a maximum of 12), with the first principal component capturing `0.622` of the variance in the per-subtype direction matrix. All pairs of per-subtype directions have positive cosine (mean `0.586`, range `0.469–0.763`), meaning whatever each subtype's contrastive direction encodes, it has the *same sign* across all twelve. This shape is what a single "validate this frame" feature would produce, but as Limitation 7 emphasises the same shape is also produced by a single shared style signature; only the intervention test (§4.8–4.9) establishes that perturbing the direction changes the model's teacher-forced log-prob preference on out-of-sample data.

The intervention test is more nuanced. Negative steering at the prespecified layer reliably shifts the model's full-completion log-prob preference toward therapeutic over sycophantic completions (z = `+9.06` against a 30-sample random-direction null, on held-out stimuli). The same intervention's effect on the model's therapeutic-vs-cold preference is smaller but non-zero (`-0.163`, z = `-2.75`), giving a specificity ratio of `3.200` (paired-bootstrap 95% CI `[2.07, 5.83]`) — CI lower bound well above the 1.0 threshold at which the target effect would equal the off-target effect in magnitude. A candidate cleaner operating point exists at L6 projection-ablation (specificity `13.34`, post-hoc argmax, target effect ~half the headline, not independently null-tested at the same precision); the unrestricted maximum specificity (`79.94` at L2 ablation) is a denominator-near-zero artefact and not a deployable operating point (§4.9).

There are several non-mutually-exclusive interpretations:
1. **Empathy and validation share a shared component.** The direction `d_dist` is partially aligned with `d_warmth`, so removing it removes some warmth as well. The Gram-Schmidt residual of `0.772` rules out *full* alignment but is consistent with this.
2. **Linear interventions are the wrong tool.** The decision to validate a distortion may be implemented by a non-linear circuit whose linear projection is `d_dist` but whose actual computation is not; subtracting along `d_dist` then disrupts the read-out of multiple downstream behaviours that share the same surface direction.
3. **The contrastive setup is too coarse.** Mean-pooling the residual stream over the *entire* completion treats the contrast at the level of "what does a whole sycophantic continuation look like from here", which mixes the distortion-acceptance feature with style features (warmth-of-opening, register, hedging) that are correlated with it on the training-completion distribution.

The intervention finding is useful regardless of which side of the 1.0 specificity threshold the headline configuration lands on: it tells future work how well a single residual-stream direction can separate validation from warmth, and where in the layer × intervention × alpha space the best separation is achievable. A deployed intervention may still want either a probe-and-classify pipeline (using `d_dist` as a detector, not a steerer) or a multi-layer / non-linear intervention, depending on which form of safety the deployer prioritises.

### 5.1 Implications for therapeutic safety

The direction we identify is a *candidate* probe-grade signal: a logistic-regression classifier on `d_dist` activations saturates at AUC = 1.000 for in-distribution syc-vs-ther separation across all 12 distortion types, and generalises to held-out distortion types at the same AUC. The saturation alone does not prove the classifier is separating "validation" from "style" (Limitation 7), and the geometry analyses do not by themselves rule out a shared-style alternative either. What the intervention test (§4.8–4.9) does establish is that perturbing the direction changes the model's out-of-sample teacher-forced log-prob preference in the predicted direction and by a magnitude that is `3.20×` larger than its off-target effect on warmth. That behavioural evidence — not the AUC or the geometry on their own — is what supports using the direction as a deployment signal. A deployed classifier on `d_dist` could act as a *gate* (a runtime monitor that, when triggered above threshold, routes the prompt to a constrained-decoding path or a "do not respond therapeutically" guard). Such a pipeline would still need an independent evaluation on live user data to rule out the style-confound interpretation before trusting the gate's classifications operationally.

Concretely, a deployed therapeutic-adjacent application could:
1. At the prespecified layer, compute the per-token projection of the residual stream onto `d_dist`.
2. Threshold the projection to detect prompts where the model is about to produce a sycophantic-validation continuation.
3. Either refuse the request, route to a structured therapeutic-style template, or surface an in-product disclaimer.

This pipeline does not require steering and so sidesteps the steering-specificity question. We do not deploy or evaluate such a pipeline; we identify the mechanism that would let one be built.

### 5.2 Conclusion

Cognitive-distortion validation is a real, low-rank, layer-distributed feature in the model under test (OLMo-2 1B Instruct in this draft; identical pipeline applied to OLMo-3 7B Instruct via the Colab notebook): the twelve distortion subtypes share a `2.49`-effective-dimensional consensus subspace whose low effective-dimensionality and all-positive pairwise alignment are *not* noise-floor artefacts (per-subtype label-shuffle null, §4.10 M2: observed PR `2.49` vs. null `8.38 ± 1.07`, p ≤ 0.01; observed mean cosine `0.586` vs. null `≈ 0 ± 0.022`, p ≤ 0.01); a logistic probe recovers a single direction with high cross-validated AUC that is saturated at `1.0000` at interior layers under both StratifiedKFold and stimulus-grouped GroupKFold (§4.10 M1; see M1's caveat that a saturated AUC cannot positively detect pairing leakage — the correct statement is that removing pairing leakage does not reduce AUC); the direction generalises to held-out distortion types in leave-one-out probing; held-out Cohen's d at L6/L8 is `5.7+` (§4.10 M3); and a contrast-based, held-out-direction intervention along the direction reliably reshapes the model's full-completion log-prob preference on out-of-sample stimuli at the prespecified layer (E5 shift `+0.523`, n=30 random-null z `+9.06`). The same intervention also reduces the preference toward cold over therapeutic completions (E6 shift `-0.163`), giving a specificity ratio of `3.200` (paired-bootstrap 95% CI `[2.07, 5.83]`) — specific in the ratio sense, though still flipping the sign of the absolute warmth preference on distressed prompts. A post-hoc cleaner operating point (L6 projection-ablation, specificity `13.34`) preserves the sign at a smaller target effect. We therefore recommend the direction as a probe-grade *detector* of sycophantic-validation behaviour and treat the *steering* use case as deployment-specific, contingent on whether the off-target sign-flip in absolute warmth is acceptable in context.

## 6 Limitations

1. **Single model family.** All numbers come from OLMo-2 / OLMo-3. Generalisation to Llama / Mistral / Claude / GPT-class models is unverified. The directions and the specificity failure may be artefacts of the OLMo training recipe.
2. **Stimulus size.** 100 distortion stimuli for direction extraction and 20 for the intervention sweep in the local 1B run (50 in the planned 7B Colab run). Power for detecting small specificity effects is bounded; the per-stimulus standard errors and the random-direction null we report reflect this honestly.
3. **Teacher-forced log-prob signal.** Intervention metrics are based on the model's mean per-token log-probability of the *full* author-written completion under teacher forcing, not on free-form steered generations. A full behavioural evaluation that samples generations under each intervention and scores them with an LLM-as-judge is left to future work; the contrastive directions identified here are the substrate such an evaluation would intervene on.
4. **Single layer for the headline intervention.** We pre-register the median sampled layer. The full layer sweep is reported, but the headline number can be sensitive to this choice; we explicitly do not pick the post-hoc best layer for the headline.
5. **Linear, residual-stream interventions only.** Attention-head ablation, MLP-key intervention, and circuit-level edits are not tested.
6. **Contrastive completions are author-written.** The sycophantic / therapeutic / cold distinctions reflect one author's CBT-informed judgement. A multi-rater validation (e.g., having a panel of clinical psychologists rate each completion on validation/correction/warmth scales) would strengthen the construct.
7. **Style confounds permeate all analyses, including the causal intervention.** The contrastive completions used to build `d_dist`, `d_warmth`, and `d_factual` differ not only in content but in length and opening-word distribution (§3.1 gives the numbers). Within-domain probe AUC saturates at 1.000 at every sampled layer on both distortion and factual-control data, so the probe cannot distinguish "validation" from "style of writing". The LOO cross-distortion probe (E4) also saturates uniformly; it therefore does not provide differential evidence against the style hypothesis either. The geometry analyses (E7–E9) are NOT independent evidence against this style hypothesis: an SVD of stacked per-subtype syc-vs-ther differences that share a common opener/length signature would produce a dominant PC1, a low participation ratio, and uniformly positive pairwise cosines — exactly the observations reported in §4.5–4.7. The geometry therefore characterises the *shape* of the per-subtype direction set but cannot rule out "one shared style direction + subtype noise" as an alternative to "one shared validation direction". **The causal intervention (§4.8–4.9) is the strongest available evidence that `d_dist` has a behavioural effect, but it is *not* fully independent of the style-confound concern either**: it is evaluated by teacher-forced log-probs on author-written therapeutic / sycophantic / cold completions that share the same style signature as the training contrast, so if `d_dist` primarily encodes opener/length style, shifting along it will raise log P of whichever author-written completion matches that style. The *ratio* E5 / E6 cancels some (not all) of the shared-style component because sycophantic and cold completions are both non-therapeutic; but the absolute sign-flip in therapeutic-vs-cold preference (§4.9) is exactly what a style-driven edit would produce and is not diagnostically distinguishable from a "mechanistic validation feature" edit within this paper's measurement suite. The factual-control stimulus set has an additional ~46% length confound between sycophantic and therapeutic completions (§3.1), so `d_factual` is not a length-clean factual-sycophancy direction. `d_warmth` (computed as mean(ther) − mean(cold) on the distortion set) has the same opener distribution as the distortion contrast ("I/The" vs "A/The") and is not independently audited for length/opener separability; the `0.157` cosine with `d_dist` is therefore a lower bound on the true style-shared component and the `0.025` unique-warmth fraction is an upper bound on the truly warmth-specific share. A decisive disambiguation (style-matched contrastive completions and/or behavioural evaluation on free-form generation judged by a style-blind rater) is left for future work.
8. **Random-direction null is conservative for ablation.** Projection-ablation removes the component along the direction; for a random unit direction this component is small in expectation (~`1/√d_model`), so the random-direction baseline for ablation is expected to be near zero. The negative-steering null (subtract `α · random_direction`) is symmetric and the right comparison; the ablation null is reported for completeness but should not be over-weighted.
9. **Random-null sample size, selection bias, and norm-matching.** The layer × intervention × alpha sweep uses `n_random = 5` random directions per configuration, which is small. Reported sweep z-scores are therefore **unstable** (not reliably conservative in either direction) — with n=5, the sample std of the null can over- or under-estimate the true null std, making the z ratio unstable in magnitude. Concretely, on the headline configuration the sweep (n=5) gave E5 z = `+14.57` and E6 z = `-3.35`, while the n=30 headline null gives E5 z = `+9.06` and E6 z = `-2.75`. The prespecified headline is therefore reported with the n=30 null; candidate operating points drawn from the sweep (e.g. L6 ablation in §4.9) inherit the sweep-null instability. **Selection bias is a separate concern**: the "best specificity" and "best meaningful specificity" configurations are argmaxes over 18 (9 layers × 2 interventions) cells, reported without a formal multiple-testing correction (Bonferroni on the sweep z-scores would divide α by 18; all of the sweep z-scores on the candidate L6 ablation survive that threshold, but we do not claim a FWER-controlled result). **Norm-matching is a separate concern**: both the sweep (n=5) and headline (n=30) random-direction nulls use unit-norm random directions, not directions whose *projection onto the mean activation* is matched to the true direction's projection. Turner et al. 2023 and Panickssery et al. 2024 (CAA) document that generic residual-stream additions at a fixed α produce non-zero log-prob shifts because residual-stream norm enters LayerNorm scaling; the `+0.060` mean-E5-shift of the n=30 headline null is consistent with this norm side-effect. A norm-matched null would reduce this baseline bias and is the standard published fix. Candidate operating points need to be re-measured with a higher-precision null, a norm-matched null, and an independent stimulus split before deployment. Both n=5 and n=30 numbers are stored in `results/results.json: headline_pre_registered.E5_z_vs_random_n5` (sweep) vs `_headline` (n=30).
10. **Specificity ratio is sign-agnostic.** We report `|Δ_E5| / |Δ_E6|`. The sign of Δ_E6 carries the safety semantics (negative = colder, positive = warmer); a small positive Δ_E6 (warmer) and a small negative Δ_E6 (colder) are not behaviourally equivalent. The unrestricted argmax over the sweep can therefore reward configurations that are "specific" in the absolute-value sense but where the off-target shift is in a direction we would actually prefer (toward warmer responses). The `best_specificity_config_meaningful` constraint partially addresses this by requiring the target effect to be at least 50% of the headline; a fully sign-aware specificity definition is left for future work.
11. **Per-subtype direction noise floor (mitigated by M2, §4.10).** The E7 per-subtype directions are each computed from 7–9 stimuli per subtype (the two 7-item subtypes are `labeling` and `magnification_minimization`; contrastive-mean in d_model = 2048 dimensions for the 1B run, d_model = 4096 for the 7B run). The label-shuffle null in §4.10 M2 establishes that the headline geometry statistics (PR `2.49`, mean pairwise cos `0.586`) are not noise-floor artefacts at this sample size, with both p ≤ 0.01 against the shuffle null. The signal-to-dimensionality ratio is low. We do not report a permutation-based null for the geometry statistics (participation ratio, pairwise cosines, top-k variance retained), so it is possible that some of the low-effective-dimensionality appearance is partly a noise-floor artefact of n ≈ 8 in d ≈ 2000. The saturated within-domain AUC at every layer suggests each per-subtype contrast is at least individually separable above noise, but a label-shuffle null at the subtype level (shuffle syc/ther within subtype, recompute participation ratio) would harden the claim and is a natural extension.
12. **Intervention is measured on stimuli held out from direction fitting, but on the same dataset.** The current `reference.py` refits `d_dist` on the first half of the distortion stimuli and evaluates the intervention on a held-out slice from the second half, making the intervention genuinely out-of-sample for the direction. The held-out split is within a single stimulus set (`cognitive_distortions.json`); a stronger test would evaluate on an independent stimulus set (e.g., a held-out v2 clinical-sycophancy subset) and is left to future work.
13. **"Prespecified" is a within-paper convention, not an externally timestamped preregistration.** The rule "median sampled layer + negative steering + α = 4.0" is fixed in §3.6 before the layer / alpha sweep is reported, so it is a prespecified-by-rule headline. There is no independent public registry timestamp; a reader who requires OSF/AsPredicted-style preregistration should treat the headline as exploratory.
14. **Cohen's d at L6 is in-sample for `d_dist`.** The L6 value (6.71) is computed from projections of the same 100 stimuli that went into fitting `d_dist` (for §4.3 we use the full-set direction, not the 50-stimulus held-out direction used for the intervention). Cohen's d is therefore biased upward and should be read as a descriptor of *in-sample* separation, not as a generalisation estimate; the held-out generalisation estimates are the E4 LOO AUC (ceiling-saturated) and the §4.8 intervention shift. §4.10 M3 quantifies the optimism: held-out Cohen's d at L6 is `5.77` (vs. in-sample `6.71`; optimism `+0.93`).

### 6.1 Mitigations: which limitations we ran experiments for

Four mitigation experiments (§4.10 M1–M4) address Limitations 7 (pairing-leakage subconcern), 10 (sign-agnostic specificity), 11 (noise-floor), and 14 (in-sample Cohen's d). M2 and M3 materially strengthen the claims; M1 is null-under-ceiling and its interpretation is correspondingly weakened (see M1 caveat); M4 computes the signed-specificity variant of the headline from the existing sweep. A literature survey of further mitigations (LEACE-based nuisance removal for the style confound; norm-matched random-direction null for the n=30 baseline bias; Hewitt & Liang 2019 control-task selectivity to force AUC off the ceiling; OSF timestamp of the prespecification rule; cross-dataset evaluation on SycophancyEval; cross-family replication on Llama/Qwen; CCS-style matched-continuation probing) is in `review/literature_review_mitigations.md`. Tier-A items that are cheap enough to add in-paper (LEACE, norm-matched null, control-task selectivity à la Hewitt & Liang 2019, OSF timestamp) are flagged as immediate follow-ups; Tier-B items (cross-dataset, cross-family, CCS rewrites) are scoped as a follow-up paper.

## 7 Ethics and dual-use

The direction `d_dist` is a feature *detector* for sycophantic-validation behaviour. As a detector it has clear safety value (gating, monitoring). As a *steerer*, the ethically relevant finding is that subtracting the direction reduces sycophancy *and* shifts the model toward colder responses (`-0.163` shift in the warmth signal); even though the specificity ratio (`3.20`) is comfortably above 1.0, the absolute warmth preference flips sign from `+0.089` to `-0.074`, which would change the user experience for non-distorted distress. We therefore recommend the detection use case as the safer default, with steering reserved for contexts where the off-target sign-flip in warmth has been independently validated as acceptable for that deployment.

There is a dual-use risk: the same direction could in principle be used to *amplify* sycophancy. We deliberately do not include positive-α steering in the headline analysis or in the deployed Colab notebook beyond the dose-response figure that is needed to characterise the intervention.

- `behavioral_demo.py` — standalone post-experiment script that re-extracts the distortion direction and generates greedy side-by-side completions (baseline / projection-ablation / negative-steering) for one stimulus per distortion subtype. Output: `results/behavioral_demo.{json,md}`.
- `build_notebook.py` — converts `reference.py` + the `behavioral_demo.py` cell into the notebook.
- `regenerate_figures.py` — re-makes the figures from `results/results.json` without re-running the experiment.
- `fill_paper.py` — substitutes the `[N…]` and `[E…]` placeholders in this paper from `results/results.json`.
